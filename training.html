How to Use a HeatWave Hair Dryer

  1. Plug in the hair dryer and turn it on by switching the power button.
  2. Adjust the heat setting based on your hair type (low for fine hair, high for thick or coarse hair).
  3. Select the airflow setting (low, medium, or high) depending on how quickly you want to dry your hair.
  4. Hold the hair dryer at least 6 inches away from your hair to prevent heat damage.
  5. Start by drying the roots, working your way down to the ends of your hair.
  6. Move the dryer in circular or downward motions to avoid heat spots and reduce frizz.
  7. If you're using a nozzle attachment, point it directly at sections of hair to focus the airflow.
  8. When your hair is dry, switch the dryer to the cool setting to help set your style and add shine.
  9. Turn off the dryer and unplug it when finished.
Troubleshooting
  1. Hair dryer won't turn on: Check if the dryer is plugged in properly and ensure the power switch is turned on. If the issue persists, check for a blown fuse or malfunctioning power cord.
  2. Hair dryer is blowing cold air: Make sure the heat setting is correctly adjusted and that the dryer isnâ€™t on the cool air setting by mistake.
  3. Hair dryer is making a strange noise: This could indicate a clog or debris in the air intake. Turn off the dryer, unplug it, and clean the vents. If the noise continues, it may require servicing.
  4. Hair dryer is overheating: Ensure the air vents are not blocked, and the dryer is not being used for prolonged periods without breaks. Overheating may also indicate an internal issue, requiring professional repair.
  5. Hair dryer is not drying hair quickly: Check if the airflow setting is too low for your needs. Try adjusting the heat and airflow settings to higher levels to increase drying speed.
